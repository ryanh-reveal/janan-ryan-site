## Money and payments

> ### Cash or card?
>
> - Card and contactless payments are widely accepted.
> - Carrying a small amount of cash can still be useful.
> - Notify your bank only if your issuer recommends it.

> ### Tipping expectations
>
> - Tipping is optional, not required.
> - Good service can be acknowledged, but there is no strict rule.

## Phones, power, and connectivity

> ### Charging and plugs
>
> - New Zealand uses Type I plugs.
> - Bring a travel adapter and check voltage compatibility.
> - Consider a compact multi-port charger for group travel.

> ### Data and maps
>
> - Roaming can work, but compare with local eSIM options.
> - Download offline maps for key neighborhoods and venues.

## Weather, health, and safety

> ### Weather reality
>
> - Conditions can shift quickly, especially in Wellington.
> - Layers and a windproof shell solve most situations.

> ### Emergency basics
>
> - Emergency services number: 111.
> - Save key accommodation and event contacts in your phone.
> - Keep travel insurance details easy to access.
